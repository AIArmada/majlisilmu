# Ilmu360 — Founder Pitch (1 Page)

> Brand note: product currently operates as **MajlisIlmu** in repository and live routes. This pitch reflects a proposed public rebrand to **Ilmu360**.

## The painful truth we are fixing

Malaysia does **not** have a shortage of masjid, surau, asatizah, or majlis ilmu.  
What we have is a shortage of **clear, reliable, timely visibility**.

People miss majlis not because they don’t care — but because info arrives late, scattered, or not at all.

That is exactly the gap Ilmu360 is built to solve.

---

## What Ilmu360 is really building

Ilmu360 is not just an event listing site.

It is a **da‘wah reliability infrastructure**:
- discover majlis quickly
- verify what is still valid
- update changes fast
- notify people on time
- let community help keep truth fresh

In short: we turn fragmented announcements into one trusted, living index.

And the name carries the mission:
- **360 discovery** (nearby, all directions, all communities)
- **360 impact** (personal, family, social, spiritual)
- **360 knowledge access** (for humanity, across languages and generations)

---

## Why “just check social media” is not enough

Social media is great for reach, but weak for operational truth:
- updates are scattered across posts/stories/groups
- changes (speaker/date/time/venue) can be missed
- old posters keep circulating
- no single source of truth for attendees

Ilmu360 solves this with structured workflows:
- searchable directory
- standardized event submission
- moderation + status labels
- reporting/correction flows
- reminders and engagement loops

---

## The cultural shift we want

From this:
- “I only go to my usual place”
- “I found out too late”
- “I’m not sure if this event is still on”

To this:
- “I can discover majlis across areas confidently”
- “I get timely updates before I travel”
- “I can bring family/friends because I trust the info”

This is more than feature usage. This is **knowledge mobility**.

It is also status and aspiration change:
- from celebrity-following culture to **ilmuwan/asatizah-centered admiration**
- from passive scrolling to active hadir, belajar, and ajak culture

---

## Why this can scale fast

Ilmu360 is designed for real local constraints:
- many masjid/surau have no dedicated IT team
- many admins are overburdened
- info quality cannot depend on one person

Our model: **community-assisted freshness** with trust controls.

Everyone can contribute. Everyone can help correct.  
Everyone can be part of da‘wah.

No dependence on one overworked admin.  
No dependence on one fragile social media account.  
Community + moderation keeps information alive.

---

## The da‘wah multiplier

One accurate event update can bring one person.
One person can bring family.
One family can influence a community.

At scale, this becomes a chain of ongoing benefit.

Ilmu360 is the channel that makes that chain easier, faster, and more consistent.

## Scale potential (conservative model)

Scenario (founder hypothesis):
- ~25,000 institutions × 1 event/month = **25,000 events/month**
- That is **300,000 events/year** before counting institutions that run multiple majlis monthly.

This is why a structured index is not optional at scale.

---

## What success looks like

- fewer missed majlis due to stale information
- faster correction of event changes
- higher repeat attendance
- more cross-area discovery (not only one local circle)
- stronger use of existing mosque/surau infrastructure

When this works, Ilmu360 becomes essential:  
**the place people trust before they go.**

## Youth breakthrough thesis

Today, many majlis are attended mostly by older generations.  
Ilmu360 can help younger audiences enter through mobile-first behavior, relevance, and social invitation loops.

Campaign seed idea:
- **“Kuliah Macam Jumaat”**
- normalize full, energetic, youth-inclusive attendance beyond Friday momentum
